**這是我對 Anthropic 與國防部事件的看法：**
**Here’s my take on the Anthropic vs. DoD situation:**


說實話，這對整個 AI 產業來說是一個巨大的警訊。作為一名長期在架構一線的人，我認為這不僅僅是合約糾紛，而是關於「技術底線」的保衛戰。
Honestly, this is a massive wake-up call for the entire AI industry. As someone deep in the architectural trenches, I see this as more than just a contract dispute—it’s a battle over the "soul" of technical boundaries.


**1. 倫理不該被標籤化為「風險」**
**1. Ethics shouldn't be labeled as a "risk"**
僅僅因為 Anthropic 堅持不將 Claude 用於大規模監視或殺傷性武器，就被標記為「供應鏈風險」？這真的有點過頭了。對於一家把「安全性」寫入 DNA 的公司來說，這種懲罰會釋放一個危險訊號：難道堅持對的事情反而會變成商業負擔嗎？
Labeling a leader like Anthropic a "supply chain risk" just because they’re sticking to their guns on safety—specifically against mass surveillance and lethal weapons—is a huge stretch. It sends a dangerous message: that having an ethical backbone is a business liability.


**2. 安全是「核心功能」，不是「外掛」**
**2. Safety is a "core feature," not an "add-on"**
從架構師的角度來看，AI 的安全框架（Constitutional AI）是系統穩定性的根基。如果你強行拆除這些安全紅線，你得到的就不再是那個可預測、可信任的模型了。我們正在討論的是損害技術本身的完整性。
From an architect’s perspective, safety frameworks like Constitutional AI aren’t just "add-ons"—they are core features of system stability. If you force a developer to strip away these guardrails, you’re compromising the integrity of the technology itself. You can’t just "patch out" ethics and expect the same level of trust.


**3. 我們需要的是合作，而不是威脅**
**3. We need collaboration, not coercion**
我支持 Anthropic 的立場。在邁向 AGI 的路上，我們需要的是「基於原則的合作」，而不是「基於壓力的服從」。如果我們現在為了短期需求而犧牲這些底線，長期來看，我們輸掉的會是整個產業的創新與人才。
I stand with Anthropic on this. As we move toward AGI, we need "principle-based collaboration," not "pressure-based compliance." If we sacrifice these redlines for short-term gains, we’re going to lose the trust—and the talent—that drives this industry forward in the long run.
